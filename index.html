Food Insecurity in New York City

Over 1.2 million experience food insecurity each year, meaning they do not always have reliable access to enough affordable, nutritious food. Organizations like Food Bank For New York City report that demand for food assistance has remained significantly higher than pre-pandemic levels, with many families relying on food pantries regularly.

Homelessness & Shelter in NYC

Each night, more than 80,000 people sleep in the New York City shelter system, including thousands of families with children. According to the New York City Department of Homeless Services, NYC operates the largest municipal shelter system in the United States, providing temporary housing, meals, and support services to those in need.
